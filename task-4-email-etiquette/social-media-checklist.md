# Social Media Do's and Don'ts

| **TYPE** | **Guideline** |
|----------|---------------|
| ✅ **DO** | Think before you post — consider how content could be interpreted by future employers, peers, or professors. |
| ✅ **DO** | Use privacy settings on all platforms to control who can see your personal information. |
| ✅ **DO** | Cite and credit others when sharing their content, ideas, or creative work. |
| ✅ **DO** | Engage respectfully in online discussions, even when you disagree with someone's viewpoint. |
| ✅ **DO** | Report cyberbullying, hate speech, or harmful content using the platform's built-in tools. |
| ✅ **DO** | Keep your professional profiles (e.g., LinkedIn) up to date and free of spelling errors. |
| ❌ **DON'T** | Share personal details such as your home address, phone number, or daily schedule publicly. |
| ❌ **DON'T** | Post or share content that mocks, demeans, or discriminates against any individual or group. |
| ❌ **DON'T** | Spread unverified information or news without checking credible sources first. |
| ❌ **DON'T** | Engage in or ignore cyberbullying — both participation and silence can cause harm. |
| ❌ **DON'T** | Use social media to vent about professors, classmates, or institutions — screenshots last forever. |
| ❌ **DON'T** | Accept follow requests or friend requests from strangers without verifying their identity. |
