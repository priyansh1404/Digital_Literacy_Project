# Stay Safe Online: Cybercrime Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Online Safety

- [ ] **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. Use a password manager (like Bitwarden) to remember them - never reuse passwords across platforms.

- [ ] **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts (email, social media, banking apps). Prefer app-based 2FA (Google Authenticator) over SMS where possible.

- [ ] **Never click on links in unsolicited SMS, WhatsApp, or email messages** claiming to be from your bank, NPCI, or government bodies. Always go directly to the official website or app.

- [ ] **Be cautious about what you share on social media** — your phone number, date of birth, and UPI ID can be used by fraudsters to impersonate you or target you.

- [ ] **Install apps only from official sources** (Google Play Store / Apple App Store). Avoid APKs from unknown links.

- [ ] **Keep your phone OS and apps updated** — security patches fix vulnerabilities that attackers exploit.

---

## Financial / UPI Safety (Mandatory Tips)

- [ ] **Understand the difference between "Send" and "Receive" on UPI.** You should NEVER enter your PIN to *receive* money - a PIN is only needed when *sending*. If anyone tells you to enter your PIN to get a refund or cashback, it is a scam.

- [ ] **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or CVV with anyone** - not even someone claiming to be a bank official, Google Pay/PhonePe support, or a government representative. Legitimate organisations never ask for these.

- [ ] **Check the collect request carefully before approving.** Always verify the name, amount, and purpose shown on screen. Reject any unexpected collect requests immediately.

- [ ] **Set a daily UPI transaction limit** in your banking app settings to minimise losses in case of a compromise.

- [ ] **Never install screen-sharing or remote-access apps** (AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport) at the request of anyone claiming to help you with a payment issue.

---

## Cyberbullying & Identity Safety

- [ ] **Review your privacy settings** on Instagram, Snapchat, and other platforms regularly. Set profiles to private and restrict who can send you messages.

- [ ] **Screenshot and preserve evidence** if you experience cyberbullying or online harassment - do not delete conversations before reporting.

---

## How to Report Cybercrime

If you are a victim of any online fraud, cyberbullying, or identity theft, report it immediately:

| Channel | Details |
|---|---|
| **National Cyber Crime Portal** | [cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in) |
| **Cyber Crime Helpline** | **1930** (Call anytime - financial fraud specifically) |
| **Local Police** | File an FIR at your nearest cyber cell |
| **Bank Helpline** | Call your bank immediately to freeze/flag the account |

> **Tip:** For UPI fraud, call **1930 within the first hour** - faster reporting improves the chance of freezing the fraudster's account before funds are withdrawn.
